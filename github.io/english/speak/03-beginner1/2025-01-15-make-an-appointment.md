#### 믿을 수가 없어 (can : 능력)

- I can't believe it!
- I can speak some English.
- I can understand a bit of English.
- I can't speak English at all.
- I can't understand English at all.
- Can you speak English?
- Can you understand some English?

#### 내 친구도 같이 가도 돼? (can : 요청과 허락)

- I can't wait (정말 기대돼)
- Can you pick the restaurant? (요청)
- Can you make the reservation? (요청)
- Can my girlfriend tag along? (허락 : 여친이랑 같이 가도 돼?)
- Can I invite a friend? (허락)
- Sure can! (그럼, 가능하지! - 캐쥬얼한표현임)
- Sorry, no can do. (미안, 안될듯! - 캐쥬얼한표현임)

#### 넌 어떤 음식 먹고 싶어?

- I'm easygoing. (난 아무거나 괜찮은 타입이야)
- I'm particular. (난 까다로워)
- Where do you want to eat?
- What do you want to watch?
- What kind of foot do you want to eat? (kind of > kinda)
- What kind of movie do you want to watch?
- Anything is fine.
- Anywhere is fine.

#### 모든 게 정말 맛있어 보여

- The ~ looks ~
  - The food looks amazing.
  - The decor looks fancy.
  - The picture looks awful. (그림이 형편없어 보여)
- This ~ tastes ~
  - My soup tastes bland.
  - This steak tastes a little overcooked.
  - My soup tastes really funcky. (funky : 이상한, 상한것처럼 이상한 맛이 나는)
  - This steak tastes a little undercooked.
- ~ sounds ~ : ~ 처럼 들려
  - That restaurant sounds really good. (그 식당 진짜 맛있을 것 처럼 들려)
  - Your mom sounds a little annoyed. (너희 엄마가 조금 짜증나신 것처럼 들려)
  - The server sounds really nice. (직원이 진짜 착한 것 처럼 들려)
